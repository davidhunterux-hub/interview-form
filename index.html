Parent Interview

Activity Discovery & Family App Validation

BEFORE DEMO

For each child, note their age and gender on a separate line (e.g., "Boy Age 6" or "Girl Age 3")

What kinds of activities do your kids currently do? (e.g., sports, arts, outdoor, unstructured play)

How do you currently find activities?

What sources do you use? (e.g., Google, Facebook Groups, word of mouth, local paper)

What frustrates you most about finding activities?

If it's a basic day, you've got nothing planned, and you want to do something with your kids spontaneously, what do you do?

How easy or difficult is it to find something spontaneous to do?

Why did you choose that number? What makes it hard/easy?

How do you keep track of activities your kids are doing or want to do?

How often do you find an activity that you'd want to do with another parent/child pair?

Any examples or context?

When you want to find other families to do activities with, how do you go about finding them?

AFTER DEMO

On a scale of 1-10 (1=not useful, 10=extremely useful), how useful do you find what you just saw?

Why that score? What made it useful or not?

On a scale of 1-10, do you think you would actually use something like this?

What would make you use it more or less?

What did you particularly like about what you saw?

What did you particularly dislike, find challenging, or find confusing?

If this was an app available today, would you download it?

Why or why not?

Do you typically download and try new apps, or not really?
